Corina Novelino (born August 12, 1912 – February 10, 1980) was a Brazilian philanthropist, writer, educator, medium, and Spiritist columnist.

Throughout her life, Novelino dedicated herself to education and philanthropic causes, contributing to the development of Spiritist literature in Brazil. She was known for her work as a columnist and medium, writing extensively about Spiritist philosophy and its application to daily life. Her work as an educator touched many lives, and she was respected in Brazilian intellectual circles for her contributions to both education and Spiritist thought.

Early life

Corina Novelino was born on August 12, 1912, in Brazil. From a young age, she showed interest in literature, education, and spiritual matters. She developed her skills as a writer and educator, eventually becoming a respected figure in Brazilian Spiritist circles.

Career

Novelino built a career as a writer and educator, contributing to Brazilian Spiritist literature through her columns and books. She was recognized as a medium and used her abilities to write about Spiritist philosophy, helping to spread knowledge about Spiritism throughout Brazil. Her philanthropic work focused on education and helping underprivileged communities.

Legacy

Corina Novelino left a lasting impact on Brazilian Spiritist literature and education. Her writings continue to be read by followers of Spiritism in Brazil and abroad. She is remembered as a dedicated educator, a generous philanthropist, and a pioneering voice in Brazilian Spiritist journalism.
