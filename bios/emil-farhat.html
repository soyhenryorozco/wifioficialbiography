Emil Farhat (1914–2000) was a Brazilian writer, journalist and marketing professional. He was born in Maripá de Minas to a family of Arab immigrant origin. He worked as a journalist and later became the head of the Brazilian branch of the American ad firm McCann Erickson.

As a writer, he won the prestigious 1988 Prêmio Jabuti for his epistolary novel Dinheiro na Estrada, which recounted the immigrant experience of his Lebanese ancestors.

Journalism and Marketing Career

Farhat built a successful career in journalism and marketing. He rose to become the head of the Brazilian branch of McCann Erickson, one of the world's largest advertising agencies.

Literary Works

  • O Paraíso do Vira-Bosta (1987)
  • Dinheiro na Estrada (1988) — Prêmio Jabuti winner
