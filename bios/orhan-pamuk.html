Orhan Pamuk (born June 7, 1952), known as Orhan Pamuk, is a turkish author. Born in Istanbul, Turkey, Orhan Pamuk won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 2006, known for novels like My Name Is Red and The Museum of Innocence that explore Turkey's cultural identity.
