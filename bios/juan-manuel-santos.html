Juan Manuel Santos Calderón (born June 10, 1951) is a Colombian politician who served as President of Colombia from 2010 to 2018. Born in Bogotá, he won the Nobel Peace Prize in 2016 for negotiating the peace agreement with the FARC.

Santos led Colombia through a historic peace process that ended decades of armed conflict.
