James David Rodríguez Rubio (born July 12, 1991), known as James Rodríguez, is a Colombian professional footballer who plays as an attacking midfielder. He is widely regarded as one of the best Colombian footballers of his generation, known for his exceptional left foot, vision, and goal-scoring ability from midfield.

James gained global recognition during the 2014 FIFA World Cup, where he won the Golden Boot as the tournament's top scorer with six goals, including the Goal of the Tournament. His performances earned him a transfer to Real Madrid.

Early life and career

James David Rodríguez Rubio was born on July 12, 1991, in Cúcuta, Colombia. He began his youth career at Envigado FC's youth academy, where his talent was quickly recognized. He made his professional debut at age 15 for Envigado in the Colombian second division.

Career

Porto (2010–2013)

James moved to Europe to join Porto in Portugal in 2010. He won multiple titles including the Primeira Liga, Taça de Portugal, and UEFA Europa League. His performances earned him the Primeira Liga Best Player award.

Monaco (2013–2014)

In 2013, James transferred to AS Monaco for €45 million. He had an impressive season in Ligue 1, scoring 10 goals and providing 14 assists, attracting attention from Europe's biggest clubs.

Real Madrid (2014–2017, 2019–2020)

After his phenomenal World Cup, James joined Real Madrid for a reported €80 million. He scored 17 goals and provided 18 assists in his first season, helping Madrid win the UEFA Champions League and FIFA Club World Cup. He later had a second spell at Madrid after his loan to Bayern.

Bayern Munich (2017–2019)

James was loaned to Bayern Munich for two seasons, winning three Bundesliga titles. He was praised for his creativity and technical skills in the Bundesliga.

Later career

After his second spell at Madrid, James continued his career at clubs including Everton (working under Carlo Ancelotti), Al-Rayyan, Olympiacos, and São Paulo.

International career

James is one of Colombia's most capped players. He represented Colombia in the 2014 FIFA World Cup (Golden Boot), 2018 FIFA World Cup, 2015 and 2019 Copa América tournaments. He is the third-highest goalscorer in Colombia's history.

Awards

  • FIFA World Cup Golden Boot (2014)
  • FIFA World Cup Goal of the Tournament (2014)
  • UEFA Champions League winner (2016, 2017)
  • Bundesliga winner (2018, 2019)
  • Primeira Liga Best Player (2012)
  • La Liga Best Midfielder (2015)
  • UEFA Team of the Year (2014)
