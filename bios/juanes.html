Juan Esteban Aristizábal Vásquez (born August 9, 1972), known professionally as Juanes, is a Colombian musician, singer-songwriter, guitarist and record producer. He is one of the most influential Latin American artists of all time, having won 27 Latin Grammy Awards and 2 Grammy Awards.

Born in Medellín, Colombia, Juanes began his music career as a member of the rock band Ekhymosis before launching a highly successful solo career. His music blends rock en español with Latin pop, addressing themes of love, social justice, and peace.

Early life and education

Juan Esteban Aristizábal Vásquez was born on August 9, 1972, in Medellín, Colombia. He grew up in a musically inclined family and began playing guitar at a young age, influenced by rock and heavy metal bands. He studied at the Universidad Pontificia Bolivariana in Medellín.

Career

Early career and Ekhymosis

In 1992, Juanes co-founded the rock band Ekhymosis, which became one of Colombia's most important rock acts. The band released several albums and gained a significant following in the Latin American rock scene. After the band dissolved in 1998, Juanes pursued a solo career.

Solo career

Juanes released his debut solo album "Fíjate Bien" in 2000, which earned him three Latin Grammy nominations. His breakthrough came with his second album "Un Día Normal" (2002), which included the massive hit "A Dios le Pido" and won five Latin Grammy Awards.

His third album "Mi Sangre" (2004) became his best-selling album, featuring international hits like "La Camisa Negra", "Para Tu Amor", and "Nada Valgo Sin Tu Amor". The album sold over 3 million copies worldwide.

International success

Juanes continued his success with albums like "La Vida... Es un Ratico" (2007), "P.A.R.C.E." (2010), "Loco de Amor" (2014), "Más Futuro Que Pasado" (2021), and "Vida Cotidiana" (2023). He has collaborated with numerous international artists and performed at major venues worldwide.

He has been recognized as one of the most influential Latin artists by Billboard, Rolling Stone, and the Latin Recording Academy.

Philanthropy

Juanes is actively involved in philanthropic work through his Fundación Mi Sangre, which supports victims of landmines and promotes peace in Colombia. He has been a United Nations Goodwill Ambassador and has participated in numerous humanitarian initiatives.
